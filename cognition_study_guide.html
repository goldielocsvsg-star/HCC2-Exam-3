Cognition Overview
Mental processes: thinking · memory · learning · judgment · decision-making
Why This Matters in Nursing
Cognitive impairment directly affects patient safety, independence, and quality of life. You must distinguish reversible from irreversible conditions quickly — your interventions and priorities differ completely.

🧩 Also Know: Pseudodementia
Depression Masquerading as Dementia

When depression causes symptoms that look like dementia — memory loss, fatigue, irritability, sleep/appetite changes. Critical nursing insight: Treat the depression → cognitive symptoms improve. Always screen for depression in apparent dementia!

Reversible Treat the depression first Cognitively improves
Alzheimer's Stages
Average disease duration: ~8 years · Usually starts after age 60
🔍 DSM-5 Criteria
Diagnosis Requires: Memory Impairment + At Least One of:
Aphasia — speech difficulty Apraxia — movement difficulty Agnosia — can't recognize objects Executive dysfunction
💊 Pharmacology
Two main drug classes — neither is a cure, both only slow progression
⚠️ Agitation Meds: Know the Rules
❌ AVOID
Haloperidol (Haldol)
↑ Risk of DEATH in elderly dementia patients. Black box warning.
✅ SAFER OPTIONS
Risperidone (Risperdal)
Quetiapine (Seroquel)
Use only when non-pharmacologic fails. Lowest effective dose.
🥇 ALWAYS FIRST
Non-Pharmacologic
Reduce noise/light · Calm communication · Consistent routine · Address unmet needs
