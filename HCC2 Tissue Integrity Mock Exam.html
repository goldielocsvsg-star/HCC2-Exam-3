Burns (ABCs + Perfusion)

  • Inhalation red flags: hoarseness/stridor/drooling/soot/singed hairs; airway can worsen fast even if SpO₂ looks okay.
  • CO poisoning: pulse ox can be misleading; check carboxyhemoglobin; treat with high-flow O₂.
  • Burn shock: capillary leak → third spacing; "puffy" ≠ adequate circulating volume. Trend UO/BP/HR/mentation.
  • Circumferential burns can restrict perfusion/chest expansion → may need escharotomy; compartment syndrome needs urgent escalation.

Pressure Injuries + Wounds

  • Blanchable redness = reactive hyperemia (not Stage 1). Nonblanchable = tissue injury risk.
  • Moist wound healing: if dry add moisture; if wet absorb (protect periwound from maceration).
  • Stage ONLY pressure injuries. MASD/friction injuries aren't staged as PI Stage 2.
  • Donut pillows + massaging red skin are traps: increase injury risk.
  • Report infection cues: new odor, purulence, warmth, increasing pain, tunneling/undermining, systemic signs.
